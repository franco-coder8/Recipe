Carbonara

Description:

A classic Roman pasta dish that boasts a luscious and creamy sauce without the use of cream. Diced guanciale or pancetta is sautéed until crispy, then combined with al dente spaghetti. The magic happens when a mixture of eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and Parmesan cheese is swiftly tossed with the hot pasta, creating a velvety coating. Seasoned with black pepper and garnished with fresh parsley, Spaghetti Carbonara delivers a flavorful and comforting experience. Each bite is a perfect balance of salty, creamy, and savory goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 200g guanciale or pancetta, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of boiling salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a skillet, cook the diced guanciale or pancetta until crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and Parmesan cheese until well combined.
  4. Heat a large pan over low heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant but not browned.
  5. Immediately add the cooked spaghetti to the pan, tossing to coat the pasta with the garlic-infused oil.
  6. Remove the pan from heat and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, tossing continuously to create a creamy sauce.
  7. Add the crispy guanciale or pancetta and toss again. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  8. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley and serve immediately.
  9. Enjoy your delicious Spaghetti Carbonara!
